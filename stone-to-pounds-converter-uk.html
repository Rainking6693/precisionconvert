UK Weight Conversion FAQ

How many pounds in a stone UK?
1 stone equals exactly 14 pounds in the UK weight system. This is the standard conversion used in British medical records, fitness tracking, and personal weight measurements. The stone has been used in Britain for centuries and remains the preferred unit for body weight.
How do you convert stone to pounds UK?
To convert stone to pounds in the UK, multiply the stone value by 14. For example, 10 stone equals 140 pounds. The formula is: pounds = stone × 14. This conversion is used throughout the UK healthcare system and fitness industry.
Why does the UK use stone for weight?
The UK uses stone for personal weight measurements as part of its traditional imperial system. Stone remains the standard for body weight in medical settings, fitness, and everyday conversation in Britain. It provides a familiar and practical unit that most Britons understand intuitively.
Do UK doctors use stone or kg?
UK doctors and NHS professionals commonly use both stone and kilograms, depending on the context. Patient records often include stone measurements for familiarity, while medical calculations may use kilograms. Many UK medical forms include both units for patient convenience.
