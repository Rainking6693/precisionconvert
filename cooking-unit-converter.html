Recipe Scaling: Multiply and Divide Like a Pro

Scaling recipes up or down requires careful attention to ratios and some ingredients behave differently at scale.

Basic Scaling Rules

  • Linear ingredients: Most ingredients scale directly (flour, sugar, liquids)
  • Spices and seasonings: Scale by 0.8x when doubling recipes (taste and adjust)
  • Leavening agents: Scale directly for small changes, adjust carefully for large changes
  • Pan size: When changing pan size, adjust baking time and temperature

Professional Chef Conversion Tips

Measurement Best Practices

  • Use a kitchen scale: Weight is more accurate than volume for dry ingredients
  • Level your measures: Use a knife to level flour and sugar in measuring cups
  • Room temperature matters: Butter and eggs behave differently at different temperatures
  • Sift when specified: Sifted flour weighs less than unsifted

Common Conversion Shortcuts

  • Pinch = 1/8 teaspoon (approximately)
  • Dash = 1/16 teaspoon (approximately)
  • Handful = 1-2 tablespoons (for herbs)
  • Splash = 1-2 teaspoons (for liquids)

Ingredient-Specific Tips

  • Honey/Molasses: Weigh instead of measuring by volume for accuracy
  • Chocolate chips: 6 oz package = 1 cup
  • Nuts: 4 oz = 1 cup chopped
  • Cheese: 4 oz = 1 cup shredded
